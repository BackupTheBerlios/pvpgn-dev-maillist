I am going to try and keep this short as possible. First off I think 
Dizzy, Aaron, and Pelish should listen to your own opinions a lot more 
strongly(bad grammar?) than anyone else on the list. Second im only 
posting here because dizzy really wanted me to post.

The goal is to get more code back into pvpgn. I only see 2 options
1) Use a license that FORCES people to give back there code legaly (GPL, 
agpl3, whatever)
Pros:
*Legally  requires someone to give back there mods.
Cons:
*It costs a lot of money to sue someone breaking the law. And if you do 
sue someone, most of the server admins do not have any money to "win" 
anyways. (right now the only servers i see using mods are eurobattle and 
some korean servers)
*It locks out business from using pvpgn. A business can not really put 
there new game into the pvpgn framework if they are required to give all 
there changes back. Maybe the business does not want to do that.
*The chances of someone giving there changes back even if its legally 
required is rare. I mean look at how many people download mp3's and 
stuff and thats "legally wrong"
*Its not really open source software IMHO because it forces people to do 
things. Pretty much the same as closed source software, forcing people 
to do things.


2) Use a license that makes it free, and hope people "do the right 
thing" (BSD)
Pros:
*Business's can still use pvpgn
*Its still free open source software IMHO.
Cons:
*its not legally required to give back changes. You have to count on 
people "doing the right thing"
*The chances of a business using pvpgn is low. The chances of a business 
giving back changes could be just as low as option 1


I guess in my personal opinion it comes down to i think the chances of 
code making there way back in pvpgn are slighly higher from a business 
than from legally scaring some server admins that probley do not speak 
english and will never even read the license. (and have no money to sue, 
and we have no money to sue)

I hate to put everything on my own shoulders but i will say i honestly 
have some plans to use pvpgn for my own game, (like planned on hopefully 
soon, within 6 months) and i would like nothing more than to built a 
video game on top of pvpgn, and keep my game server code closed, but 
still give back changes to pvpgn and bug fixes. This happens ALL the 
time btw in the BSD community. Companys base there products on Freebsd 
for example and when they find bugs and stuff they give back patches, 
"just because".


-Ender
